# Design System Document: The Editorial Juris

## 1. Overview & Creative North Star
**Creative North Star: "The Authoritative Curator"**

This design system moves away from the "template-heavy" look of traditional legal websites. Instead of rigid grids and boxed-in sections, we embrace an **Editorial High-End** aesthetic. The goal is to convey the gravity of legal services through sophisticated tonal layering, intentional asymmetry, and a "breathing" layout. 

By utilizing the "Authoritative Curator" approach, we treat information as premium content. We break the standard landing page flow by using overlapping elements and high-contrast typography scales, ensuring the user feels they are in the hands of a modern, tech-forward legal elite rather than a bureaucratic institution.

---

## 2. Colors & Surface Philosophy
The palette is anchored in deep oceanic blues (`primary`) and high-energy gold (`secondary`), balanced by a sophisticated neutral scale.

### The "No-Line" Rule
**Strict Mandate:** Designers are prohibited from using 1px solid borders to define sections. Layout boundaries must be created solely through:
1. **Background Color Shifts:** Moving from `surface` to `surface-container-low`.
2. **Tonal Transitions:** Using `surface-container-highest` to anchor a call-to-action against a `surface` background.

### Surface Hierarchy & Nesting
Treat the UI as a physical stack of premium paper. 
- **Base Layer:** `surface` (#f9f9ff) for the widest areas.
- **Content Anchors:** `surface-container-low` (#f0f3ff) for secondary informational blocks.
- **High-Focus Elements:** `surface-container-lowest` (#ffffff) for primary cards to create a "lifted" feel against the cooler background tones.

### The "Glass & Gradient" Rule
To escape the "flat" look, use Glassmorphism for floating contact widgets or navigation bars.
- **Spec:** Background `surface-container-lowest` at 80% opacity with a 20px backdrop-blur.
- **Signature Textures:** Apply a subtle linear gradient (135deg) from `primary_container` (#032956) to `primary` (#001532) for Hero sections to add depth and "soul."

---

## 3. Typography
We utilize **Inter** (as the functional evolution of the brand's DM Sans/Telegraf mix) to provide a clean, hyper-readable experience across all scales.

*   **Display (lg/md):** Used for Hero headlines. Tracking should be set to -2% to feel "tight" and editorial.
*   **Headline (lg/md):** Used for section headers. These are the anchors of authority.
*   **Title (lg/md):** For card headings and testimonial names.
*   **Body (lg/md):** The workhorse for legal explanations. Line height should be generous (1.6) to ensure clarity for stressed users.
*   **Label (md/sm):** For micro-copy and "Trust Signals" (e.g., "OAB Verified").

---

## 4. Elevation & Depth
Hierarchy is achieved through **Tonal Layering**, not shadows.

*   **The Layering Principle:** To highlight a testimonial, place a `surface-container-lowest` card on top of a `surface-container-low` section. The contrast in "cleanliness" creates a natural visual lift.
*   **Ambient Shadows:** If a floating action button (FAB) or high-priority lead form requires a shadow, it must be: `Blur: 40px`, `Opacity: 6%`, `Color: on-surface` (#151c27).
*   **The "Ghost Border" Fallback:** If accessibility requires a container definition, use `outline-variant` (#c4c6d0) at **15% opacity**. Never use a 100% opaque border.
*   **Glassmorphism:** Use for "floating" trust signals (like "5-star rating" badges) to let the brand colors bleed through, creating an integrated, modern feel.

## 5. Components

### Buttons (The "Action" Drivers)
- **Primary:** Background `secondary_container` (#fee001), Text `on_secondary_container` (#716300). Bold, 135deg subtle gradient. Use `rounded-md` (0.375rem).
- **Secondary:** Background `primary`, Text `on_primary`. For "Learn More" or secondary legal paths.
- **Tertiary:** Text `primary`, no background. Used for "Cancel" or "Back" actions.

### Input Fields (The "Trust" Points)
- **Styling:** `surface-container-lowest` background with a `Ghost Border`.
- **Focus State:** Transition the border to `surface-tint` (#425f8e) and add a 4px soft outer glow of the same color at 10% opacity.
- **Error State:** Use `error` (#ba1a1a) for helper text and a subtle `error_container` tint for the field background.

### Cards & Lists (The "Evidence" Blocks)
- **Rule:** **Strictly forbid divider lines.** 
- Use **Spacing Scale 8** (2.75rem) to separate list items or use alternating background shifts (`surface` to `surface-container-low`).
- **Cards:** Must use `rounded-xl` (0.75rem) to feel approachable yet modern.

### Additional Signature Components
- **Trust Bar:** A full-width `surface-container-highest` bar with grayscale logos of partners or media mentions, using `outline` (#747780) at 50% opacity.
- **Progressive Disclosure Accents:** Vertical accent bars (3px wide) using `secondary` (#6c5e00) to the left of "Headline-md" text to anchor the eye.

## 6. Do's and Don'ts

### Do
- **Use Intentional Asymmetry:** Offset your hero image from the headline to create a "custom coded" feel.
- **Prioritize Whitespace:** Use `spacing-20` (7rem) between major sections to reduce the user's cognitive load during legal stress.
- **Use Tonal Depth:** Always place lighter containers on darker backgrounds to guide the eye toward the "light" (the solution).

### Don't
- **Don't use "Legal Blue" gradients:** Avoid the 90s-style bright blue gradients. Stick to the deep, "Midnight" tones of the `primary` scale.
- **Don't use generic icons:** Avoid thin-line icons. Use weighted, duotone icons that utilize `primary` and `secondary` colors.
- **Don't box the content:** Avoid "Container" wrappers that are visible. Let the content breathe to the edges of the grid.
- **Don't use 100% Black:** Always use `on_surface` (#151c27) for text to maintain a premium, soft-contrast look.
